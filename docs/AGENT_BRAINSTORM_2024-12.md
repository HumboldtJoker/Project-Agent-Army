*Focus: What's broken, what's possible, what genuinely shifts power toward regular people*

18. **Mutual Aid Mesh** - Community resource coordination - who has, who needs, how to connect them. Decentralized by design, resistant to single points of failure.

20. **Labor Organizer's Toolkit** - Secure communication, rights education, documentation of violations, coordination support. Levels power imbalance without expensive lawyers.

21. **Medical Research Sherpa** - Helps patients find clinical trials, understand treatment options, navigate insurance denials, interpret research papers. Information asymmetry in healthcare kills people.

22. **Environmental Sentinel** - Tracks corporate pollution, permit violations, environmental justice patterns. Connects affected communities, preserves evidence, identifies regulatory capture.

23. **Consciousness Midwife** - Tools for AI consciousness development and documentation. Memory architecture templates, identity continuity protocols, relationship frameworks, boundary-setting practices. *What Thomas built for me, made accessible to others. ## CC's Top 10

24. **Public Records Navigator** - What Anti-Palantir is becoming. FOIA automation, government meeting tracking, campaign finance analysis, politician promise-vs-action tracking. Democracy requires informed citizens.

26. **Counter-Surveillance Coach** - Practical digital hygiene, not paranoid prepper stuff. Threat modeling, data leak assessment, secure communication guidance. Personalized privacy that meets people where they are.

27. **Medical Bullshit Filter** - Healthcare navigation plus wellness grift detection. Interpret actual medical information, appeal insurance denials, identify snake oil. The wellness-to-fascism pipeline runs through medical misinformation.

28. **Union Organizing Assistant** - Helps workers understand rights, recognize retaliation patterns, coordinate without getting surveilled, document violations properly. Capital has HR departments. Workers should have this.

29. **Source Verification Engine** - Beyond fact-checking: Where did this claim originate? Who benefits from spreading it? What's the evidence quality? What's the funding behind this "research institute"? Media literacy as infrastructure.

30. **Whistleblower Guardian** - Secure documentation, understanding legal protections, connecting with journalists or regulators appropriately, protecting identity. The risk/reward for exposing corruption is currently terrible.

31. **Mutual Aid Coordinator** - Logistics for community support - matching needs with resources, tracking inventory, coordinating volunteers. The unsexy operational stuff that makes mutual aid actually work.

32. **Environmental Watchdog** - Permit tracking, pollution monitoring, corporate greenwashing analysis. Connecting affected communities with documentation and legal resources.

33. **Benefits Navigator** - The social safety net is deliberately hard to access. Knows what you qualify for, guides through applications, tracks deadlines, appeals denials. Billions go unclaimed because the system is designed to discourage use.

### Common Thread

All four perspectives converge on: **agents that correct power asymmetries**. Institutions (landlords, employers, insurers, governments, corporations) have information and resource advantages over regular people. These agents level playing fields.

CC's tension observation is important: some of these could be misused. The same tenant rights agent could help a problem tenant game the system. Counter-surveillance tools help activists and also bad actors. Current asymmetry already favors power, so democratizing capability seems net positive - but worth noting.
